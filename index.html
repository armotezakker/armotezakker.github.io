Equity Factor Model: S&P 500 Multi-Factor Strategy
Built a walk-forward quantitative strategy on S&P 500 stocks using momentum, low volatility, and value signals. Backtested 2019 to 2024 with quarterly rebalancing, no lookahead bias, and transaction costs at 0.1% per trade. Combined portfolio returned +21.8% CAGR vs +17.2% for SPY. Factor attribution showed momentum dominated this cycle at +39.7% CAGR standalone — an honest but uncomfortable finding about multi-factor diversification.

Sentiment Alpha Signal: FinBERT vs Dictionary Methods
Compared a transformer-based sentiment model (FinBERT) against a Loughran-McDonald finance dictionary on 3,164 news headlines across 15 stocks. FinBERT found a statistically significant correlation with next-day returns that survives Bonferroni correction, but a placebo falsification test showed the same signal correlates almost equally with past returns, an honest, ambiguous result that could reflect either reactive news coverage or a genuine contrarian signal. Built with proper multiple-comparisons correction throughout.
